Frequently Asked Questions

What is high-risk car insurance? +

High-risk car insurance is designed for drivers who insurance companies consider more likely to file claims. This includes drivers with traffic violations, at-fault accidents, DUI convictions, or those who require SR-22 filings. These drivers typically pay higher premiums due to their increased risk profile.

How much does SR-22 increase insurance costs? +

An SR-22 filing can increase insurance costs by 50-150% on average. The exact increase depends on your state, driving history, and insurance company. An SR-22 is not insurance itself but a certificate proving you carry the state-required minimum insurance. Insurance companies view drivers requiring SR-22 as high-risk, leading to significantly higher premiums.

How can I lower my high-risk insurance rates? +

You can potentially lower your rates by: (1) Maintaining a clean driving record going forward, (2) Taking defensive driving courses, (3) Increasing your deductible, (4) Bundling multiple insurance policies, (5) Shopping around with multiple providers annually, (6) Considering usage-based insurance programs, and (7) Improving your credit score (in states where it's allowed).

What factors affect high-risk insurance costs most? +

The primary factors are: Driving record (recent violations and accidents), age (especially under 25), location (urban vs. rural), type of vehicle, coverage limits required, length of continuous insurance coverage, credit history (in most states), and specific violations (DUI increases rates more than speeding tickets).
