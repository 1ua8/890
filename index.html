Frequently Asked Questions

What is high-risk car insurance? +

High-risk car insurance is for drivers who are considered more likely to file a claim. This includes drivers with a history of accidents, traffic violations, DUIs, or those who require an SR-22 filing. Insurance companies may charge higher premiums for these drivers.

Does SR-22 increase insurance cost? +

Yes, an SR-22 filing typically increases insurance costs. An SR-22 is a certificate of financial responsibility required for high-risk drivers. Insurance companies view drivers requiring SR-22 as higher risk, which results in higher premiums.

How can I lower my insurance rate? +

You can potentially lower your insurance rate by maintaining a clean driving record, taking defensive driving courses, increasing your deductible, bundling insurance policies, and shopping around for quotes from multiple insurance providers.

What factors affect high-risk insurance costs? +

Key factors include driving record (accidents, tickets, DUIs), age, location, type of vehicle, coverage limits, credit history (in some states), and how long you've been continuously insured.
